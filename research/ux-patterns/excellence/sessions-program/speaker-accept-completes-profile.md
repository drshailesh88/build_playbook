# Pattern: Accept-the-role = complete-the-profile in one CTA

**Surface:** sessions-program / speaker-lifecycle · **Observed in:** Substack (refs: [guest-author invite flow](https://mobbin.com/flows/32f0c028-f7bc-4418-b27e-c47183b9bffd))

## Flow
1. Invite link lands on "Set up your profile" — name (required), handle, bio, with field-level WHY hints ("Tip: this will be shown at the bottom of your guest post").
2. One combined CTA: "Update Profile & Accept Invite" — profile completion and role acceptance are a single action.
3. Done state sets expectations: "We'll let you know when your guest post is live — Your profile is set up and good to go!"

## Use when
The role being accepted NEEDS profile data downstream (speaker bio/photo on the public program). Collecting it at the moment of maximum motivation kills the post-accept "please send us your bio" chase.

## Avoid when
The invite must convert in seconds (simple session RSVP) — a long form before "accept" depresses acceptance rates. Keep accept one-click and request the profile immediately after, on the confirmed page.

## Sad paths observed
- "Already a writer? Switch accounts" — wrong-account escape hatch on the invite form itself.

## Accessibility
Single-column centered form; required marker on the one truly required field only.

## Microcopy worth stealing
"Update Profile & Accept Invite" · per-field purpose hints explaining where the data will appear.

## Default verdict for our stack
RECOMMENDED (post-accept variant) — faculty accept should stay one-click, then the confirmed state immediately asks for bio/photo "shown on the public program" while the speaker is still on the page. Solves the speaker-profile data gap that blocked old-app deferred item D5 (speaker tap-through profiles).
